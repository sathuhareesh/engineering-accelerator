4 years.
45 days to start right.

Not a course about grades or placements. A personal guide to becoming someone who thinks clearly, builds things, and knows where they're going.

"Every day you should become a better version of yourself than yesterday."

When I graduated from IIT Kharagpur, nobody handed me a map. Here's what I had to figure out the hard way:

  • Grades matter less than what you build with your time
  • Placement is not the destination — becoming someone interesting is
  • The real curriculum is never in the syllabus
  • These 4 years are a platform, not a tunnel — use them to find yourself

I wasted mine playing video games while my classmates built robots and discovered who they were. This is the roadmap I wish I had at 18.

— Sathu Hareesh Babu, CSE · IIT Kharagpur

📓
Keep a notebook through these 45 days. Write what resonates — even one sentence per day. Handwritten or digital, your choice. Go at your own pace. This is not a race. If Day 12 takes you three days, that's perfectly fine.
💬 Engineering can get overwhelming. If you ever feel anxious or stressed — talk to a friend, call home, reach out to your college counsellor. Asking for help is not weakness. It's wisdom.
⚠️ If you skipped or rushed through the Python days — bookmark this page. You will come back. Every engineer, at some point, needs to code. The foundation you skip now is the gap you'll scramble to fill later. Go back and do it seriously — even just 30 minutes a day.

Resources for the Journey

Curated picks to watch, read, and listen to alongside this course.

3 Idiots
Bollywood
The most important Indian film about engineering. Watch it on Day 1 and again on Day 45 — you'll see it completely differently.
Chhichhore
Bollywood
About college friendships, failure, and what it all means. Don't let anyone spoil the ending.
The Pursuit of Happyness
Inspiration
When you're tempted to quit anything. Watch this. Will Smith's best performance.
The Social Network
Tech / Entrepreneurship
How Facebook was built in a dorm room. Flawed characters, brilliant ideas, and a crash course in startup culture.
The Intern
Work & Life
About age, experience, and what "being useful" really means. Surprisingly wise.
Dead Poets Society
Classic
"Carpe diem — seize the day." About the courage to live your own life, not the one others plan for you.
Good Will Hunting
Classic
Genius wasted without guidance and belief. A reminder that potential alone is not enough — you need people who push you.
Rocket Singh: Salesman of the Year
Bollywood
An underdog Indian film about building something honest in an imperfect system. Criminally underrated.
Ikigai
Self Discovery · Start Here
Short and beautiful. About finding what makes your life worth living. Perfect for Day 1 of engineering.
Atomic Habits
Habits & Consistency
The definitive book on building good habits. Pairs directly with Day 4 of this course.
The Alchemist
Fiction / Philosophy
A short novel about following your curiosity. Reads in one evening and stays with you for years.
Deep Work
Focus & Productivity
How to do focused, distraction-free work in an age of constant noise. Essential for engineers.
Zero to One
Entrepreneurship
Peter Thiel on building companies that create something genuinely new. Challenges every assumption about "success."
Rich Dad Poor Dad
Money & Mindset
How money actually works — the things school never teaches. Read before you get your first salary.
Who Moved My Cheese?
Adaptability · Short Read
A 90-minute read about adapting to change. Sounds simple. The lesson hits hard.
The Hard Thing About Hard Things
Entrepreneurship · Advanced
Ben Horowitz on building companies in chaos. Read this in Year 3 or 4 when you're ready for it.
WTF is with Nikhil Kamath
Indian Podcast · YouTube
Nikhil Kamath interviews India's most interesting entrepreneurs, athletes, and thinkers. Start with any episode that interests you.
Lex Fridman Podcast
Tech / Science / Philosophy
Long, deep conversations with scientists, engineers, and founders. Every episode is a masterclass.
The Knowledge Project
Mental Models · Thinking
Shane Parrish interviews the world's best decision-makers. Pairs perfectly with Day 39 of this course.
The Seen and the Unseen
India / Ideas · Hindi/English
Amit Varma's podcast on India's most important ideas — economics, politics, culture. Brilliant for building a global perspective.
Paul Graham's Essays
Blog · paulgraham.com
Start with "What You'll Wish You'd Known." Written for students. One of the most important things you'll ever read.
Wait But Why
Blog · waitbutwhy.com
Tim Urban writes the most entertaining long-form essays on life, career, and how to think. Start with "Your Life in Weeks."
Farnam Street Blog
Blog · fs.blog
Mental models, decision-making, and learning how to learn. One of the best thinking resources on the internet.

10 years ago, I graduated from IIT Kharagpur in Computer Science and stepped into the world feeling underprepared — not technically, but as a person. Nobody had talked to me about how to think, how to manage my time, what to read, or who I wanted to become. This course is the conversation I wish someone had started with me on Day 1. If it helps even one student use these 4 years more intentionally, it's worth every word.
